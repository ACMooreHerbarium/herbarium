NOTES Thurs., April 17

Polygonaceae (Buckwheat Family)

  • stipules fused to form ocreae (sing. = ocrea)
  • perianth 6 sepals in 2 whorls
  • superior ovary
  • fruit an achene, trigonous (triangular) or lenticular (lens-shaped)
  • inner 3 sepals often adnate to achene
  • most synoecious, some (ex. Rumex) dioecious

Onagraceae (Evening Primrose Family)

  • inferior ovaries
  • hypanthium often extending far beyond the ovary
  • fruit a capsule
  • flowers often 4-merous, 5-merous in Ludwigia
  • many are moth-pollinated, the flowers open at night
  • comose seed: one equipped with a tuft of hairs ("coma")
