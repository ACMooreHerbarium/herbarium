I. Rosaceae

  • leaves stipulate, simple or compound
  • fruit a drupe, pome, follicle, achene, (rarely a capsule), or an aggregate
  • hypanthium or receptacle sometimes ripening with the fruit
  • ovaries superior or inferior
  • carpels distinct or connate

    • subfamilies:

    • Rosoideae
      herbaceous or woody, superior ovary, fruit an aggregate of achenes or drupelets, sometimes with accessory hypanthium or receptacle - ex. dewberry
    • Prunoideae
      woody plants, superior ovary, fruit a drupe - ex. plum
    • Maloideae
      woody plants, inferior ovary/ovaries, adnate hypanthium - ex. apple
    • Spiraeoideae
      woody, capels usually distinct, ovaries superior, fruit an aggregate of follicles (rarely a capsule)

II. Violaceae

  • mostly tropical; it is there that most of the diversity in this family exists
  • flowers 5-merous (5 petals, sepals, stamens), strongly bilateral to nearly actinomorphic
  • 3-carpellate
  • short filaments, anthers appressed to the ovary
  • stipulate
  • two temperate genera:
    • Hybanthus (green violet)
    • Viola
      - fls strongly zygomorphic, one petal spurred
      - short filaments, lower 2 stamens sometimes bearing nectaries
      - fruit a capsule
      - chasmogamous and cleistogamous flowers (except in one species - V. pedata)
      - leaves simple, often with dramatic stipules
      - garden pansy is a member of this genus
