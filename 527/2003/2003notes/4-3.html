NOTES Thursday 3 April

I. Ericaceae (heath family)

  • tropical and temperate distributuion, about 3500 species
  • Lyonia mariana - urceolate corolla, actinomorphic, very short lobes, superior ovary
  • Vaccinium spp. (blueberries) inferior ovaries, actinomorpohic corolla
  • Rhododendron spp. (azaleas) superior ovary; corolla lobes well-developed; corolla irregular (bilateral)
  • anther dehiscence is porate
  • Fruit a capsule or berry
  • Monotropoideae- a subfamily containing fleshy herbs, generally without chlorophyll, and most likely to be saprophytic. Example: Monotropa uniflora ("Indian pipes")

II. Apiaceae = Umbelliferae (carrot family)

  • mostly temperate parts of the world
  • ovaries always inferior
  • much variation in leaf dissection, often with sheathing petioles
  • fruit schizocarpic
  • often have stylopodia
