Notes March 18

I. Fabaceae (Leguminosae)

  • fruit a legume
  • leaves often compound
  • alternate leaves
  • stipules
  • subfamilies:
      A. Papilionoideae
      • zygomorphic flowers with banner, wings, and keels
      • banner/standard outside wings
      • temperate as well as tropic
      • ex. Vicia angustifolia ("Vetch")
      B. Caesalpinoideae
      • zygomorphic flowers with banner, wings, and keels
      • banner/standard inside wings
      • mostly tropical/subtropical
      • ex. Cercis canadensis ("Redbud")
      C. Mimosoideae
      • flowers actinomorphic
      • showy stamens
      • flowers often in short spikes or heads
      • mostly tropical/subtropical
      • ex. Albizia julibrissin ("Mimosa")

II. Liliaceae

  • is divided by some taxonomists into a number of smaller, separate families
  • fruit usually a capsule or berry
  • monocot - things in threes, parallel veins
  • many with very showy flowers and planted as ornamentals
  • includes garlic, onions, daffodils
  • ex. Ipheon uniflorum
