I. Fruits from one pistil (simple fruits)

    A. fleshy fruits

    • berry (many-seeded)
    • drupe (one-seeded)
    • pome (rose family...ovary + hypanthium)...
    • hesperidium - berry, with leathery rind and oil glands...orange family (Rutaceae)
    • pepo - berry with hard shell(ex. gourd)

    B. dry fruits

    INDEHISCENT
    • achene - single-seeded, seed and pericarp attached only by ovule stalk
           • samara - winged achene
           • cypsella - achene with adnate calyx (pappus), ex. Taraxacum
    • caryopsis - grain (single seed, seed coat + pericarp completely adnate). Limited to grasses.
    • nut - one-seeded, hard exocarp, often subtended by an involucre, ex. acorn + cup

    DEHISCENT

    • follicle - 1 carpel, one seam
    • legume - 1 carpel, 2 seams
    • capsule - 2 or more carpels, opens to release seeds. Can dehisce in a variety of ways, generally with 2 or more seams.
           • silique - specialized capsule, 2-carpellate, dehiscing along 2 seams, falling away from a central papery membrane. Found only in Brassicaceae.
           • silicle - short (less than 2x as long as wide) silique
           • circumcissile capsule - dehiscing along a horizontal ring, like jar + lid
    • schizocarp - not a kind of capsule. Fruit does not break open to release seeds; instead fruit breaks into 1-carpellate sections (mericarps) that fall away with seeds inside. (If the mericarps also dehisce, releasing the seeds, then it becomes a schizocarpic capsule.)
      • nutlet - breaks into one-seeded, HALF-carpellate segments. (a mericarp is a complete carpel.)

II. fruits from more than one pistil

  • aggregate fruits (more than one pistil, but all from a single flower) - mass of simple fruits from an apocarpous gynoecium; each pistil matures into one simple fruit. ex: blackberry, strawberry, magnolia
  • multiple fruits - one fruit from several individual flowers. ex. Maclura, fig

Families

I. Lamiaceae = Labiatae - mint family

  • square stems
  • always opposite leaves
  • often highly zygomorphic, bilabiate corollas
  • stamens 2 or 4, often 4 and didynamous (2 long, 2 short)
  • 4-lobed ovary produces 4 nutlets (or less, if some ovules are abortive)
  • includes many aromatic culinary herbs, such as mint, rosemary, marjoram, and oregano
  • also includes common weeds such as Lamium amplexicaule
  • traditional name: Labiatae

II. Brassicaceae = Cruciferae - mustard family

  • alternate leaves
  • four petals
  • four sepals
  • estipulate
  • inflorescence a raceme
  • stamens often (but not always) six, 4 long + 2 short
  • fruit a specialized 2-carpellate capsule called a silique or silicle
  • many contain sulphur compounds
  • mustard, kale, collards, turnips, and broccoli are all members of this family.
  • many mambers of this family have a peppery flavor, such as radish and horseradish.
  • traditional name: Cruciferae
