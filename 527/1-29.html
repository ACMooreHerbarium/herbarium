GYMONOSPERMS
-"naked seed"
-do not produce flowers or fruits
-most do not have vessel elements in xylem
-much more diverse and and abundant in the fossil record, esp. Mesozoic, than today
-much preceeded appearance of angiosperms

Conifers
- seeds/pollen borne in cones/strobili
- Pinaceae (Pine family)
     - woody ovulate cones/ephemeral pollen cones
     - two seeds per scale, often winged
     - resinous
     - often dominant in high latitudes and mountains
- Cupressaceae (Cypress family)
     - some taxonomic treatments include Taxodiaceae within Cupressaceae
     - Juniperus (red cedar) with fleshy, berry-like cones, is a member

Cycads
     - Cycadaceae
     - Zamiaceae
     - ovules borne in strobili or on the margins of modified leaves
     - palm-like appearance, popular as ornamentals

Gnetophytes
     - Ephedra

Ginkgoaceae
     - one extant species, Ginkgo biloba
     - dioecious
     - seeds with fleshy, stinky outer coating - NOT a fruit, but resembling one
     - fan-like leaves with bifurcating veins
